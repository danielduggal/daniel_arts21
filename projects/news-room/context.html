The story of "GME to $1,000"
Posted by u/DanielDuggal      2 weeks ago

CONTEXT
This story centers around a corner of the web called r/wallstreetbets...

But this phenomenon is the culmination of several factors, including:
  1. smartphones - have given people access to the world in the palm in their hand
  2. the internet and its culture - has created infinite places for people to gather, share ideas, and coordinate action. This is best expressed by social media paltforms and the resulting human behavior - memes, trolling, influencers, fake news, social justice campaigns, capital hill attacks, etc
  3. electronic and complex financial markets - over the last 30 years financial markets have become digital. This has made it much easier to buy or sell any security (a tradable financial asset) at any time. In addition, financial markets have become much larger and much more complex. In particular, derivatives, which are a more complex and risky way to make investments, have grown exponentially
  4. financial technology innovation - in this case, it is about Robinhood. But many other companies and products over the last couple decades have contributed to making it easier and cheaper for anyone to invest
  5. the pandemic - has left people stuck at home and bored. Betting on the stock market with some friends from the internet using an app on our phones seems like a reasonable enough way to pass the time. Especially when stocks are going up and up


Ultimately, this Gamestop phenomenon is a rorschach test (inkblot test) and everyone will have their own take. The above explanation is not comprehensive but is hopefully directionally correct...
